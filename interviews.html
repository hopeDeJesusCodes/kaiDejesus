Interviews

Video

Yngvie Malmsteen

WSOU Radio

Kai DeJesus-- or as they're known on-air, DJ DJ-- talks with Yngvie Malmsteen, a neoclassical guitarist, about his inspirations, the other instruments he plays, and the impact of classical music on his artistry. Malmsteen is a solo musician ranked as one of the top ten greatest electric guitarists of all time in Time Magazine.

Michael Sands

Extra Life Stream 2022

Just before the release of the Codex of Worlds Expansion Kickstater, Kai DeJesus talks to Monster of the Week creator Michael Sands about its inception. Monster of the Week is an urban fantasy-horror tabletop roleplaying game that emulates stories like CW'S Supernatural and Buffy the Vampire Slayer. It was featured on critically acclaimed actual play podcast The Adventure Zone.

This interview was presented by the Seton Hall Gaming Sector as part of their Extra Life Charity Stream in Fall 2022. Extra Life benefits Children's Miracle Network Hospitals, funding pediatric treatments and other health services.

Amit Moshe

Extra Life Stream 2022

Kai DeJesus talks diverse gaming and queerness with Amit Moshe, creator of City of Mist and Queerz!. Both Queerz! and City of Mist focus on humanity and culture, leaning on themes of internal struggle, empathy, and identity. Forbes recognized City of Mist as one of the best tabletop roleplaying game starter boxes of 2021.

This interview was also presented by the Seton Hall Gaming Sector as part of their Extra Life Charity Stream in Fall 2022.

Rev Deschain

Extra Life Stream 2022

Kai DeJesus talks about tabletop games that aren't Dungeons and Dragons (no hate!) and what it takes to make an actual play show with Rev Deschain, the game master for The Critshow. The Critshow a multi-show podcast featuring games like Monster of the Week, City of Heroes, Trophy Dark, and more. Deschain is also the Emmy nominated co-host of PBS show Journey Indiana.

This interview was also presented by the Seton Hall Gaming Sector as part of their Extra Life Charity Stream in Fall 2022.

Audio Interviews

Strix Beltran

SHU Undergraduate Leaders Podcast

Kai DeJesus discusses diversity in gaming and leading through stories with Strix Beltran, creator of hit tabletop roleplaying game Bluebeard's Bride. She also has credits in games such as Urban Shadows and Dungeon and Dragons Expansion books like Van Richten's Guide to Ravenloft and Journey Through the Radiant Citadel.

This interview was conducted with the help of The Seton Hall Undergraduate Leaders Podcast. You can find more information under Projects.

Walt Way

SHU Undergraduate Leaders Podcast

Kai DeJesus talks family ties and making art with Walt Way, creator of the Brister English Project. The Brister English Project seeks to reconnect descendants of black slaves with their family trees through research. Way is also a poet and author.

This interview was also conducted with the help of The Seton Hall Undergraduate Leaders Podcast. You can find more information under Projects.
